Trade Flows and Competition Categories

About TFCC

Trade Flows and Competition Categories have the task of identifying trade flows and determining whether they belong to a price or non-price (quality) group of categories with one-way trade flows.

Gehlhar & Pick (2002) introduced a method for two-way trade flows that aims to separate price and non-price (quality) competitiveness, after Bojnec & Fertő (2008) introduce two more categories, introduce categories for one-way trade flows. During the development of the software, shortcomings were identified, cases recognized by the software, and five new categories were introduced to avoid them.


Calculation method:
Trade balance
Export unit value
Import unit value
Unit value difference


Gehlhar & Pick (2002) introduced categories; K2, K3, K9 and K10. Bojnec & Fertő (2008) introduced one-way trade flows and they are marked by categories; K1 and K11. During the development of the software, Uzunović & Mešić introduced categories; K4, K5, K6, K7 and K8.
